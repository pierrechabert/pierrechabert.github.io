Coastal Ocean Dynamics, Ecosystems and Climate Extremes

Research interests

I am interested in how the coastal ocean behaves and responds to different atmospheric processes. A key aspect of my research concerns Eastern Boundary Upwelling Systems, which are specific coastal ocean domains where biological production is disproportionate to the surface they occupy in the ocean, often associated with important socio-economic activities. I am interested in biological-physical interactions, especially in how the ecosystem is structured by oceanic currents after upwelling, which is a facet CCE-LTER related initiatives (). Another aspect of my research is to understand how synoptic (5-10 days) wind fluctuations impact upwelling dynamics and the associated coastal planktonic ecosystem, focusing on the processes involved (as part of the SOLAB project ). My ongoing research focus on climate extremes that drive sea level extremes on the east coast of North America as part of the 3CRS project in Emanuele Di Lorenzo and Baylor Fox-Kemper groups. I mainly use high-resolution regional dynamical (CROCO, SCHISM) and marine biogeochemistry (PISCES) models.
